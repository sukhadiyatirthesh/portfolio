## 1. The Power of Broad Targeting (Let the Algorithm Work)

Gone are the days of hyper-granular interest targeting. Today, the Meta algorithm is smarter than any manual targeting structure you can build.

By using **Broad Targeting**, you give Facebook's machine learning the maximum amount of data to find your ideal customer. The key here is not the targeting settings, but the **Creative**. Your creative (video or image) acts as the filter. If your ad speaks directly to business owners looking for B2B Lead Gen, only they will stop scrolling and engage.

### Actionable Steps:
- Combine all your small lookalike audiences (1-3%) into one large 0-10% lookalike.
- Run completely open targeting (No interests, just Age and Location).
- Let the ad copy and video hook do the filtering.

## 2. Advantage+ Shopping Campaigns (ASC)

If you run an eCommerce brand, ASC is non-negotiable. Meta's Advantage+ Shopping Campaigns use advanced AI to automate the entire campaign creation process, testing hundreds of creative and audience combinations simultaneously.

However, the mistake most media buyers make is putting *all* their budget into ASC. It should be used alongside your proven business-as-usual (BAU) campaigns.

### The ASC Strategy:
1. **Budget Cap for Existing Customers:** Always set a budget cap (e.g., 10-15%) for existing customers in ASC to ensure you are driving *new* acquisition, not just retargeting people who were going to buy anyway.
2. **Creative Diversity:** Feed the ASC campaign a mix of User Generated Content (UGC), static images, carousel product catalogs, and professional videos. 

## 3. Advanced Conversion API (CAPI) Integration

With the decline of third-party cookies, relying solely on the Meta Pixel is a recipe for disaster. You must implement the **Conversions API (CAPI)**.

CAPI sends web events directly from your server to Facebook's server. This ensures that even if a user has an ad blocker or iOS tracking prevention enabled, the purchase or lead event is still tracked and attributed correctly. 

**Without CAPI, you are feeding the algorithm incomplete data**, which leads to higher CPAs (Cost Per Acquisition).

## 4. Creative Testing at Scale

Creative is the new targeting. You need a systematic approach to testing creatives without disrupting your main scaling campaigns.

Use a dynamic creative testing (DCT) campaign structure:
- **Campaign Objective:** Sales or Leads.
- **Ad Set Structure:** Broad targeting.
- **Dynamic Creative:** On. Add 3 videos, 2 primary texts, and 2 headlines.
- **Budget:** Strict daily budget to force the algorithm to spend.

Once a creative proves its worth by hitting your target CPA consistently over 3-4 days, extract the Post ID and inject it into your main scaling campaign to retain the social proof (likes and comments).

## Frequently Asked Questions (SEO FAQs)

**What is a good ROAS for Meta Ads?**
A "good" ROAS depends entirely on your profit margins. For some businesses, a 2X ROAS is highly profitable, while others need a 4X ROAS just to break even. Focus on your Break-Even ROAS and scale from there.

**Is broad targeting better than interest targeting?**
In 2026, broad targeting generally outperforms interest targeting at scale because it gives Meta's machine learning algorithm a larger pool of users to optimize against, leading to lower CPMs and CPAs.

**How much should I spend on Meta Ads per day?**
You should aim to spend enough to exit the "Learning Phase" within 7 days, which requires 50 conversion events. If your target CPA is $20, you need a minimum budget of $142/day ($20 x 50 / 7 days) at the ad set level.
